UJUZIworks with vocational education institutions, skills service providers and training institutions nationwide to advocate for access, quality and relevance of skills training.
UJUZI conducts dialogue and carries out research with private sector companies, development partners and government to advocate for skills development.

Access to skill

We work in projects that look at the availability and accessibility of skills particularly to women, youth and people from marginalized community. Although there is an increasing tendency to enrolment to schools for many African countries, the numbers of people who are able to gain access to skills (in the education system and other institutions) are still statistically low.
UJUZI is conducting numerous research to gather information on the (in) accessibility to skills, the results of which will inform on the projects proposal to donors for implementation in order to improve accessibility of skills in Tanzania

Quality of Skills

Current research shows that biggest percentage of employers are not satisfied with the graduates’ practical skills. In sub Saharan Africa, graduates possessing formal credentials lack relevant skills to perform their job adequately. Quality is also a concern in basic education, mirrored by the low level of basic knowledge of those entering secondary education in many African countries. UJUZI does research with employers to gather data on the skills demanded and informs skills providers on the quality of skills needed.

Relevance of Skills

To underscore the linkage between access and quality of skills, data shows that the low employment rate among graduates of technical training is a result of market relevance of the skills provided for many countries in Sub Saharan Africa, Tanzania included. In Tanzania, soft skills demanded by the employers were only recently loosely introduced in the curricula. There is apparent mismatch between the skills provided to the youth in the education institutions and those needed by the industry.
UJUZI through research and projects carries out studies and assessment to gather the relevance of the skills given by skills providers, and linking the results of the projects and research to employers and other social partners.
